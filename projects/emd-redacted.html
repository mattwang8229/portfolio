Capital-Raising Strategy — Exempt Market Dealer

Strategy & Operations · KPI design · Investor materials · Confidential / Redacted

Overview

To reduce third-party fees and improve control over investor relations, I contributed to a proposal for an in-house Exempt Market Dealer (EMD). This page summarizes my approach and deliverables without disclosing proprietary data or client identifiers.

Problem

  • External dealers increased costs and fragmented investor visibility.
  • Limited control over the investor journey, reporting cadence, and relationship management.
  • Need for a compliant, scalable model aligned with exempt distribution rules.

My Role

  • Co-authored business-plan outline (strategy narrative, operating model, go-to-market).
  • Defined KPIs and dashboard concepts for pipeline, conversion, retention, AUM growth, and fees saved.
  • Drafted investor-facing summaries and an executive one-pager; designed presentation artifacts.
  • Mapped process & controls across the investor lifecycle in alignment with exempt market requirements.

Approach

  • Market & Operating Scan: target investor profiles, distribution pathways, capability gaps.
  • Operating Model & Workflow: roles, RACI, onboarding/KYC, reporting, comms.
  • Metrics & Reporting: KPI definitions and wireframes to monitor performance & compliance readiness.
  • Execution Roadmap: licensing, governance, tooling, communications rollout.

Outcome

The deliverables provide a structured path to internalize capital raising, improve investor experience, and establish a metrics-driven reporting cadence. All materials shown here are sanitized; concrete numbers and client identifiers are intentionally omitted.

Confidentiality: Process and frameworks I created or contributed to are shown; proprietary data is excluded.
